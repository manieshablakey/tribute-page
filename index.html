Fifteen Fascinating Facts About Bees:

  1. There are nearly 20,000 known species of bees.
  2. Bees are found on every continent except Antartica.
  3. Approximately one third of the food we eat is the result of honey bee pollination
  4. A colony of bees consists of 20,000-60,000 honey bees. There are three types of bees in every hive: a queen, worker bees and drones.
  5. The queen bee can live up to 5 years and is the only bee that lays eggs. A productive queen can lay up to 2,500 eggs per day.
  6. Worker honey bees are female, live for about 6 weeks and do all the work.
  7. Only drones are male. They are larger than the worker bees, have no stinger and do no work. All they do is mate.
  8. The bees’ buzz is the sound made by their wings which beat 11,400 times per minute.
  9. One bee has to fly about 90,000 miles – three times around the globe – to make one pound of honey.
  10. The average bee will make only 1/12th of a teaspoon of honey in its lifetime
  11. Bees have 2 stomachs- one for eating and one for storing nectar.
  12. Honey lasts an incredibly long time. An explorer who found a 2000 year old jar of honey in an Egyptian tomb said it tasted delicious!
  13. Bees can be trained to locate buried landmines.
  14. Honey bees communicate with one another by dancing.
  15. If the queen bee dies, workers will create a new queen by selecting a young larva (the newly hatched baby insects) and feeding it a special food called royal jelly. This enables the larva to develop into a fertile queen.
